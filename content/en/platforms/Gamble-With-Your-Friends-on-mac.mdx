## Bankroll control, settlement etiquette, and trust management

Social wagering works only when trust stays intact. The fastest way to lose a group is sloppy settlement behavior.

### Personal bankroll framework (simple and realistic)

Use a monthly recreational budget, then split it into weekly units. Keep each wager to 1–3 units. This reduces emotional tilt after losses and discourages “chasing.”

| Bankroll element | Example amount | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Monthly fun budget** | **$200** | Pre-set and non-essential money only |
| **Weekly allocation** | **$50** | Stop when weekly cap is hit |
| **Unit size** | **$5** | Most wagers = 1–2 units |
| **Max single bet** | **$15** | No impulsive oversized plays |

### Settlement etiquette checklist

- Pay promptly within agreed window  
- Confirm receipt with screenshot or message  
- Don’t renegotiate after results finalize  
- Keep side commentary friendly, not personal  
- Pause play if someone is uncomfortable with limits

When you **Gamble With Your Friends on mac**, etiquette is as important as picks. A clean process protects relationships better than any winning streak.
