## Recording and Editing Workflow for Viral Reels

Don’t wait until editing to “find the story.” Tag moments live while recording sessions.

### Step-by-step production pipeline

| Stage | Action | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Session Setup** | Record all POV audio + one clean game feed | Full match archive |
| **Live Marking** | Drop timestamps when chaos starts | Clip shortlist |
| **First Cut** | Build 30–60 sec rough cut | Story skeleton |
| **Reel Cut** | Trim to 8–35 sec vertical | Final social clip |
| **Packaging** | Add subtitle punch-ins + cover frame | Publish-ready post |

### Editing rules that improve retention

- Start with outcome or conflict first, then flash back 2–4 seconds.
- Keep dead air under 0.5 seconds.
- Use hard subtitles with speaker labels in group clips.
- Zoom punches on reaction beats.
- End on unresolved tension when possible (“Part 2?”).

> **Warning:** Don’t post context-heavy clips without a setup caption. In team chaos games, confusion kills completion rate.

A practical upload stack for **Gamble With Your Friends instagram** growth:
- **3 Reels/week**
- **2 Stories/day** (polls, blame votes, clip teasers)
- **1 carousel/week** (“best/worst plays of the session”)

## Common Mistakes (and Fixes) for New Creators

If growth is flat, it’s usually packaging, not gameplay quality.

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Long intro before action | Drop-off in first 3 sec | Open on peak reaction |
| No subtitles | Silent viewers skip | Burn in high-contrast captions |
| Random clip order | No narrative payoff | Use setup → chaos → result |
| Too many inside jokes | New users feel excluded | Add 1-line context in caption |
| Inconsistent posting | Weak account momentum | Batch edit and schedule weekly |

> **Tip:** Build a recurring series title (example: “Casino Disaster Report #07”). Series consistency increases profile follows.

For creator teams specifically: assign roles.
- **Clip captain:** timestamps and shortlist
- **Editor:** final reels + covers
- **Community lead:** replies, polls, and story follow-ups
