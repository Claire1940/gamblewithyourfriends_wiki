## Update Your Graphics Drivers

One of the most common reasons for visual hitches is an outdated GPU driver. Manufacturers like NVIDIA and AMD frequently release "Game Ready" drivers that include specific optimizations for multiplayer titles. A proper **Gamble With Your Friends stuttering fix** almost always begins with a clean driver installation.

1.  Identify your GPU model (Right-click Start > Device Manager > Display Adapters).
2.  Visit the official manufacturer website (NVIDIA, AMD, or Intel).
3.  Download the latest stable release for 2026.
4.  Perform a "Clean Installation" if the option is available to remove old, corrupted files.
5.  Restart your computer to finalize the changes.

## Administrative Privileges and Fullscreen Optimizations

Windows sometimes throttles background processes or limits the resources available to a game if it isn't recognized as a high-priority task. Running the game as an administrator can bypass these restrictions.

1.  Navigate to the game's installation folder (usually under `SteamLibrary\steamapps\common`).
2.  Right-click the game's `.exe` file and select **Properties**.
3.  Go to the **Compatibility** tab.
4.  Check the box for **Run this program as an administrator**.
5.  Check the box for **Disable fullscreen optimizations**.
6.  Click Apply and OK.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Disabling fullscreen optimizations can sometimes cause issues with overlays (like Discord or Steam Overlay). If your stuttering stops but your overlay disappears, you may need to choose which feature is more important for your session.

## Optimize Windows Power and Gaming Settings

Your operating system might be trying to save energy, which can lead to a significant drop in clock speeds for your CPU and GPU. For a permanent **Gamble With Your Friends stuttering fix**, you should ensure your PC is set to maximize performance.

*   **Enable Game Mode:** Press the Windows Key, type "Game Mode Settings," and toggle it to **On**. This prevents Windows Update from installing drivers in the background and limits background activity.
*   **High Performance Power Plan:** Open the Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Power Options. Select the **High Performance** or **Ultimate Performance** plan.
*   **Hardware-Accelerated GPU Scheduling:** In Windows 11 settings, go to System > Display > Graphics > Change default graphics settings. Turn on **Hardware-accelerated GPU scheduling** to reduce latency.

## Adjusting In-Game Settings for Stability

While we all want the game to look its best, certain settings are notorious for causing frame-time spikes. If you are still seeking a **Gamble With Your Friends stuttering fix**, try these specific adjustments within the game's video menu:

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on Stuttering |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **V-Sync** | Off | High (Reduces input lag) |
| **Frame Rate Limit** | Match Monitor Refresh | Medium (Prevents GPU spikes) |
| **Anti-Aliasing** | FXAA or Off | Low (Reduces GPU load) |
| **Texture Quality** | Medium | Medium (Saves VRAM) |
| **Window Mode** | Fullscreen | High (Better resource priority) |

If you are playing on a laptop, ensure that the game is actually using your dedicated GPU (NVIDIA/AMD) rather than the integrated Intel/AMD graphics. You can force this in the [NVIDIA Control Panel](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/geforce/geforce-experience/) or Windows Graphics Settings.

## Clear Cache and Temporary Files

Over time, temporary files and shaders can accumulate, causing the engine to struggle when loading new assets. Clearing these files can act as a secondary **Gamble With Your Friends stuttering fix**.

1.  Press `Win + R`, type `%temp%`, and delete the contents of the folder.
2.  In the Steam client, go to Settings > Downloads > **Clear Download Cache**.
3.  Restart Steam and log back in.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does Gamble With Your Friends keep stuttering even on a high-end PC?**

A: Even powerful PCs can suffer from stuttering if there are software conflicts. Common causes include aggressive anti-virus scans, background recording software (like ShadowPlay or OBS), or corrupted shader caches. Applying a **Gamble With Your Friends stuttering fix** like disabling overlays or updating drivers usually resolves this.

**Q: Will lowering my resolution fix the freezing issues?**

A: Lowering resolution reduces the load on your GPU. If your stuttering is caused by reaching 100% GPU utilization, then yes, it will help. However, if the stutter is caused by CPU bottlenecks or slow hard drive speeds, changing the resolution may have little effect.

**Q: Is the stuttering caused by my internet connection?**

A: While "lag" usually refers to network latency, it can often feel like stuttering. If other players are teleporting or your inputs are delayed, it is likely a network issue. If the entire screen freezes or the frame counter drops, it is a hardware performance issue.

**Q: Does running the game in Windowed Borderless mode help?**

A: Generally, "Fullscreen" mode offers the best performance because it gives the game exclusive control over the display buffer. Windowed Borderless is convenient for alt-tabbing but can introduce "Desktop Window Manager" latency, which contributes to micro-stuttering.
