### Roulette Strategy: The Red/Black Cycle
Roulette is the most reliable way to complete floor challenges. Many challenges require you to "profit $120 on roulette," which can be achieved through a simple doubling strategy. However, the game often features "streaks" where one color dominates for several spins.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Assessment">
    Check the history board to see the last five winning colors. If a color has appeared three times in a row, the odds of a switch are statistically higher.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Base Bet">
    Start with a bet that is 10% of your current bankroll. This allows for at least three "doubles" if you hit a losing streak.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Pivot">
    If you lose on Black, immediately double your bet and stay on Black. Switching colors mid-loss is the fastest way to drain your team's funds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Cashing Out">
    Once you hit your profit target for the floor challenge, leave the table. Greed on roulette is the primary cause of team wipes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The High-Stakes Duck Race">

Duck Racing is the "all-or-nothing" mechanic of the game. Unlike other games where you simply place a bet, Duck Racing requires mechanical input that can determine whether you become a billionaire or lose everything.

When you enter a Duck Race, you aren't just betting on a color; you are betting on your ability to "charge" the duck. Players must hold the left-click button to increase their duck's speed, but overcharging will cause the duck to "break," resulting in an instant loss.

<Callout type="success" title="Winning the Duck Race">
The sweet spot for charging is usually when the ducks reach the 75% mark of the track. If you hold it too early, you'll burn out. If you hold it too late, you won't have enough momentum to pass the leaders.
</Callout>

**Duck Racing Tiers:**
- **Farooq**: High speed, high break chance.
- **Earlop**: Balanced, reliable for mid-tier bets.
- **Yard**: High durability, slow start.

Items like the **Max Coin** can be used here to bypass the standard entry fee, allowing for "All-In" bets that can skip entire floor quotas in a single race. However, as seen in many professional runs, the Duck Race is often where the most promising runs come to a tragic end.

<Section icon={Map} title="Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them">

Many teams fail not because of bad luck, but because of poor decision-making. The casino environment is designed to trigger impulsive behavior.

1. **Chasing Losses**: If you lose a large bet on the Wheel, do not immediately go All-In on the next spin. Take a moment to regroup and use a Profit Book to rebuild slowly.
2. **Ignoring Shark Challenges**: These challenges are the only way to get enough tickets for the Max Coin. Even if the challenge seems difficult (like betting $850 on a single spin), the reward is usually worth the risk.
3. **Splitting Up Too Much**: While it's good to check different machines, staying within voice range is crucial for coordinating All-In bets and item usage.

<Callout type="warning" title="The Time Machine Trap">
The Time Machine item reverts your winnings as well as your losses. Many players use it thinking they can keep their money while undoing a bad bet, but it resets the entire game state. Only use it if a teammate is about to be killed by the shark.
</Callout>
