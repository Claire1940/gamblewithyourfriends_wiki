<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your Machine">

Setting up a multi-card session requires a bit of preparation. You don't want to play the previous player's "cold" numbers. Follow these steps to ensure your machine is fresh and configured for your specific strategy.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Slate">
    Insert your player's card and cash. Select "Multi-Card Keno" and use the "Erase All" or "Clear" button to remove any existing numbers from cards A through J.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Set Your Denomination">

  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Input Your Patterns">
    Select Card A and draw your Flying V. Select Card B and draw your Cluster. Repeat this for as many cards as you wish to play, using overlapping numbers to increase hit probability.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adjust Game Speed">

  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Start the Draw">

  </Step>
</Steps>

Not all Keno machines are created equal. When you **gamble with your friends keno**, you must look at the paytable *before* you start betting. While this sounds nice, it usually results in a significantly lower jackpot for hitting all your numbers.

Casinos also monitor these machines closely. Historically, Keno balls were audited using programs (some developed at MIT) that tracked the frequency of every ball over 1,000+ games. If a single ball, like #34, appeared in more than 33% of games, the square would turn red on the auditor's screen, signaling that the ball might be weighted or chipped and needs replacement.
