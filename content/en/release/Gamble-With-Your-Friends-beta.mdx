## Smart Bet Design: How to Create Fair, Actionable Wagers

Most players lose edge through poor bet wording. A good wager is specific, measurable, and settleable without debate.

### The “clear outcome” formula

Use this template:

- **Subject:** What exactly is being predicted?
- **Condition:** What must happen?
- **Time window:** By when?
- **Data source:** Where is it confirmed?
- **Tie/void rule:** What if conditions are unclear?

**Example (strong):**  
“Team A wins in regulation time (no overtime) on 2026-05-10, result verified via official league box score. If match postponed 24+ hours, wager void.”

**Example (weak):**  
“Team A crushes Team B tonight.”

### Bet type comparison

| Bet Type | Skill Requirement | Volatility | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Straight outcome** | **Low–Medium** | **Low** | Beginners | Easiest to settle |
| **Prop bet** | **Medium** | **Medium** | Intermediate groups | Fun but needs precise wording |
| **Parlay-style social picks** | **High** | **High** | Risk-seeking players | Use small stakes only |
| **Live micro-bets** | **Medium–High** | **High** | Active watchers | Great in chat-heavy sessions |

In **Gamble With Your Friends beta**, props and live bets are fun, but they can inflate variance fast. If your goal is leaderboard climbing, straight outcomes with consistent volume usually perform better over time.

> **💡 Tip:** When creating custom wagers in Gamble With Your Friends beta, write settlement criteria first, then write the headline. Clarity beats hype.
